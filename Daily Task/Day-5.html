About Nature

Nature encompasses all living things and physical phenomena,
functioning as our life-support system. It provides essential resources
(air, water, food), sustains ecosystems, and offers mental health
benefits. Valuing these natural assets is critical for human survival
and prosperity.

Why Protecting Nature Matters

This article is published on: july 09, 2026

Conserving the environment is not just about
saving wildlife; it is fundamentally about self-preservation. When we protect natural habitats, we support global food security
and stabilize the climate. The importance of maintaining our "natural capital" is
championed by major conservation groups like the World Wildlife Fund (WWF) and
The Nature Conservancy.



Spectacular Waterfalls

A waterfall is a steep drop in a river where water flows over a rocky ledge into a
pool below. It is primarily formed by erosion,
where the water wears away soft rock faster than hard rock,
eventually causing a cliff to form and retreat upstream.
